Monkeys

Monkeys are a group of primates, which are mammals that are characterized by their advanced cognitive abilities and dexterous hands. There are over 260 known species of monkeys, and they can be found in a variety of habitats, including tropical rainforests, savannas, and grasslands.

Types of Monkeys

  • New World Monkeys: These monkeys are native to Central and South America, and they have a shorter snout and a prehensile tail that they use to grasp objects. Examples include capuchin monkeys and squirrel monkeys.
  • Old World Monkeys: These monkeys are native to Africa and Asia, and they have a longer snout and no prehensile tail. Examples include macaques and baboons.

Behavior and Diet

Monkeys are social animals and live in groups called troops. They communicate with each other through vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language. Monkeys are omnivorous and eat a variety of foods, including fruits, nuts, insects, and sometimes small mammals.

Conservation Status

While some monkey species have stable populations, others are threatened or endangered due to habitat loss, hunting, and other human activities. Conservation efforts are underway to protect these animals and their habitats.
